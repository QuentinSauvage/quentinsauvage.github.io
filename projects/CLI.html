Command-line Interpreter

Project link | October 2018 - December 2018

This Interpreter was made as a school project during my first year of Master. It implements several UNIX commands the user can type in the shell and understands wildcards, pipelines and redirections and handles jobs in background or foreground. It also reacts to signals, such as Ctrl-C. Its purpose was to confirm my OS and C knowledge, specifically concerning multi-threading, shared memory, signals handling as well as processus handling.

Among the features directly implemented, the interpreter contains status, cd, ls, ps, jobs, bg and fg. Other commands can still be executed by the interpreter, using the default implementation, and the processus will still be handled by the program. Creation, modification and use of local or environment variables are also taken into account.
